1 Executive Summary

APP fraud losses across UAE licensed financial institutions (LFIs) increased by 38.4% year-on-year in 2024, rising from AED 412M to AED 570M. Mobile banking emerged as the dominant channel, accounting for 63% of APP fraud losses. sq1

Authorised push payment fraud was concentrated among six LFIs, which together accounted for 57% of sector-wide APP losses despite representing only 21% of total digital transaction volume. Three of these institutions — EIB, ABB, and FNB — exhibited APP fraud rates more than 2.5× the peer median for the full year. sq4

Supervisory attention is warranted for institutions with high mobile-channel exposure and below-median detection rates, particularly given the sharp Q4 surge observed sector-wide. sq6

2 APP Fraud Volume & Year-on-Year Trends

Total reported APP fraud losses increased from AED 412.3M (2023) to AED 570.7M (2024), representing a 38.4% YoY increase. The growth rate accelerated in H2 2024, with Q4 2024 alone recording AED 178M in losses — the highest single quarter on record. sq1

3 Channel Analysis

Mobile banking is the primary APP fraud vector in 2024, accounting for 63% (AED 360M) of total sector losses. Internet banking accounted for 27%, with telephone banking and branch at 10% combined. The mobile channel share increased from 54% in 2023, consistent with growing digital adoption across UAE LFIs. sq2

The mobile channel concentration is particularly elevated at EIB (79%), ABB (74%), and RAK (71%), all of which fall in the top-6 outlier group. This suggests that weak mobile-specific fraud controls may be a common factor among high-loss institutions. sq2 sq4

6 Recovery Rates

The sector-wide APP fraud recovery rate in 2024 was 11.4% — meaning only AED 65.1M of the AED 570.7M lost was recovered. This is below the GCC peer benchmark of 15–18%. Recovery rates were significantly higher at institutions with dedicated fraud operations teams (avg. 19.2%) vs. those relying on shared AML-fraud units (avg. 7.8%). sq5
